About

Who I am

I'm an aerospace engineer working at the intersection of classical space engineering and modern AI. My background covers astrodynamics, numerical methods, and control systems — and over time my research has shifted toward autonomous space systems.

Specifically, I care about how you get guidance, navigation, and control to work reliably when a spacecraft has to make decisions on its own — far from Earth, under uncertainty, without the luxury of waiting for ground commands.

The thread connecting most of my work is deployability. A system that works in theory but can't run on real hardware isn't finished. I care about closing that gap.

Alongside research, I work as a Multibody Engineer at PoliSpace, building high-fidelity simulation models for rover subsystems in MATLAB, Simulink, and Simscape.

Selected work

Master's Thesis · 2024–2026
Reinforcement Learning for Autonomous Interplanetary Guidance
↗

Hybrid AI pipeline combining optimal control, behavioural cloning, and PPO for autonomous low-thrust Earth–Mars guidance. Validated onboard an NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano via Processor-in-the-Loop testing — sub-millisecond inference latency, robust to missed-thrust events and actuation noise.

Reinforcement Learning Optimal Control PyTorch PIL Validation Astrodynamics
Interplanetary Trajectory Design
↗

Optimized Mars-to-asteroid trajectory using Lambert's problem and powered Earth gravity assist, minimizing ΔV with MATLAB-based numerical algorithms. Validated orbit propagation under J2 and lunar perturbations.

MATLAB Lambert's Problem Trajectory Optimization
LEO Microsatellite Attitude Dynamics & Control
↗

MATLAB/Simulink control system for a Sun-synchronous microsatellite. Implemented B-dot and PD-based non-linear control, achieving precise Earth-pointing with robust sensor/actuator models.

Simulink GNC Attitude Control
Spacecraft Guidance
↗

Computed Earth–Moon Lagrange points and halo orbits in CRTBP with 10-digit accuracy. Optimized a two-impulse transfer trajectory and solved low-thrust orbit raising with Pontryagin's Maximum Principle, achieving final state errors below 10⁻⁸ km.

MATLAB SPICE Optimal Control CRTBP
Spacecraft Navigation & State Estimation
↗

Compared uncertainty propagation methods (LinCov, Unscented Transform, Monte Carlo) for Earth–Moon transfer. Developed batch least-squares and Unscented Kalman Filters to track ESA's SMOS satellite.
